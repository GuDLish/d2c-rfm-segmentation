# Manual Review Cases

This document highlights customer cases that may require additional manual business review.

---

# High Spending but Low Frequency Customers

## Description
Customers who spend large amounts but purchase infrequently.

## Possible Risks
- Competitor switching
- Seasonal purchasing behavior
- Declining engagement

## Recommended Actions
- Personalized outreach
- Premium customer support
- Product preference analysis

---

# Loyal Customers Showing Reduced Activity

## Description
Historically active customers who recently became less engaged.

## Possible Risks
- Early churn signals
- Reduced customer satisfaction
- Changing product preferences

## Recommended Actions
- Re-engagement campaigns
- Customer satisfaction surveys
- Personalized offers

---

# Recently Acquired High-Value Customers

## Description
New customers with unusually high spending patterns.

## Possible Risks
- One-time purchasing behavior
- Temporary promotional activity

## Recommended Actions
- Retention-focused onboarding
- VIP targeting campaigns
- Personalized recommendations

---

# Customers with Inconsistent Purchase Patterns

## Description
Customers showing unstable or irregular purchasing behavior.

## Possible Risks
- Unstable engagement
- Low long-term retention probability

## Recommended Actions
- Purchase behavior analysis
- Marketing interaction review
- Campaign optimization

---

# Final Recommendation

Customers identified in these categories should be periodically reviewed by marketing and customer success teams to improve retention and maximize customer lifetime value.
