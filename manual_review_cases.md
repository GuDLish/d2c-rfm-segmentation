# Manual Review Cases

## Overview

Automated customer segmentation systems cannot fully capture all customer behaviors and business complexities.

Certain customers display conflicting or ambiguous signals that require additional manual investigation before retention decisions are made.

This document highlights important customer-review scenarios identified during the customer intelligence analysis.

---

# Why Manual Review Matters

Traditional segmentation systems may incorrectly classify customers because:

- historical spending does not always reflect future behavior,
- churn signals may conflict with RFM scores,
- engagement patterns may change rapidly,
- operational dissatisfaction may remain hidden,
- and customer intent is not always observable through transactions alone.

Manual review helps identify high-risk customers that automated scoring may overlook.

---

# Case 1 — High-Value but Low Engagement Customers

## Characteristics

- High Monetary value
- Historically strong purchase behavior
- Low recent sessions or declining activity

## Business Risk

These customers may appear valuable based on historical transactions while silently disengaging from the platform.

Traditional RFM scoring alone may incorrectly classify them as healthy customers.

## Recommended Review

Investigate:

- inactivity duration,
- browsing decline,
- product interaction trends,
- and recent engagement behavior.

## Suggested Action

Prioritize proactive engagement recovery before complete churn occurs.

---

# Case 2 — Loyal Customers with Negative Support Sentiment

## Characteristics

- Strong purchase frequency
- Negative support interactions
- Reopened complaints or long resolution times

## Business Risk

Operational dissatisfaction may weaken long-term retention despite strong historical purchasing behavior.

These customers may churn unexpectedly if service issues remain unresolved.

## Recommended Review

Investigate:

- unresolved complaints,
- repeated support interactions,
- escalation history,
- and refund-related patterns.

## Suggested Action

Prioritize service recovery before promotional retention campaigns.

---

# Case 3 — Recently Acquired High-Spending Customers

## Characteristics

- Very recent activity
- High monetary value
- Limited historical purchase behavior

## Business Risk

Short-term spending spikes may not represent stable long-term customer value.

These customers may still be evaluating the brand.

## Recommended Review

Investigate:

- acquisition source,
- engagement consistency,
- repeat-purchase intent,
- and early retention behavior.

## Suggested Action

Focus on onboarding quality and long-term relationship building.

---

# Case 4 — Churned Champions

## Characteristics

- Previously classified as Champions
- High historical RFM scores
- Confirmed churn behavior

## Business Risk

These cases demonstrate limitations of relying entirely on historical purchase data.

High-value customers may still churn due to disengagement or dissatisfaction.

## Recommended Review

Investigate:

- engagement decline timeline,
- support dissatisfaction,
- competitive behavior,
- and inactivity progression.

## Suggested Action

Build early-warning systems for behavioral decline among valuable customers.

---

# Case 5 — Low Monetary but High Engagement Customers

## Characteristics

- Low purchase value
- High session activity
- Frequent product interaction

## Business Risk

These customers may represent future growth opportunities despite currently low spending.

Automated segmentation systems may undervalue them.

## Recommended Review

Investigate:

- browsing consistency,
- wishlist/cart behavior,
- and product-category engagement.

## Suggested Action

Use personalized recommendations and conversion-focused campaigns.

---

# Case 6 — Customers with Conflicting Behavioral Signals

## Characteristics

Examples include:

- high engagement but low purchases,
- high purchases but declining sessions,
- positive spending but negative support sentiment,
- or frequent visits with long inactivity gaps.

## Business Risk

Conflicting signals increase uncertainty in automated segmentation and churn prediction systems.

## Recommended Review

Investigate customer behavior holistically instead of relying on a single metric category.

## Suggested Action

Combine transaction, engagement, support, and churn signals before assigning retention investment.

---

# Key Strategic Observation

The analysis demonstrates that customer intelligence systems should not rely solely on traditional RFM scoring.

Customer behavior is multidimensional and may require:

- behavioral interpretation,
- operational context,
- churn analysis,
- and manual investigation

for accurate retention decision making.

---

# Conclusion

Manual-review workflows help identify:

- hidden churn risks,
- dissatisfied high-value customers,
- silent disengagement,
- and ambiguous behavioral patterns

that automated segmentation systems may fail to detect.

These cases are important for improving retention prioritization accuracy and reducing strategic decision errors.s
